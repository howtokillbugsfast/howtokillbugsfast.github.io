How to Kill

DUST MITES

1. Identify Dust Mites

Dust mites are tiny, microscopically-sized arthropods (8-legged). They are approximately 0.2mm to 0.3mm in size, so they are not detectable to the naked eye. They survive off of our dead skin cells, which can feed around 1 million dust mites in one day!

Dust mites are often the cause of allergic reactions, in varying degrees. Their feces contain allergen that can result in coughing, sneezing, watery eyes, congestions, facial pressure, and even asthma attacks. If you suspect that you are experiencing a dust mite allergy, visit your local allergist to get tested.

WHERE DUST MITES ARE FOUND

Dust mites can be found in almost every home. With a large proportion of the US population having some sort of allergic reaction or sensitivity to dust mites, effective management and control of the situation is necessary. 

Dust mites flourish in warm and humid environments. They are most comfortable in areas that are 68ºF to 78ºF with a 75 to 85 percent humidity, although they survive in anything over 50 percent humidity. They can burrow deep in fabrics and carpeting, meaning that they are often found in rugs, mattresses, pillows, and other upholstered furniture.

Because dust mites are so small, any kinds of disturbances such as walking, sitting, and vacuuming, can cause them to become airborne. They can stay in this state for up to two hours.

2. Treat the Dust Mite Infestation

 1.     Use a dehumidifier or air conditioner. Since dust mites cannot survive in environments of 50% humidity or less, using a humidifier is an easy and effective way in stopping reproduction and survival of dust mites. Keep the humidity level at 35% or less for at least 16 hours a day for effective management of dust mites. It is recommended that you use a frequently cleaned HEPA (high efficient particulate air) filter for the best results.

2.     Heat treat your items. In the case of your launderable items, you can easily heat your clothes and linens to kill and wash off dust mites from your belongings. Simply load your items into the wash and soak them for at least 15 minutes in hot water (53ºC).

3.     Steam clean your carpets and furniture. Heat is the way to go when treating surfaces around your home. Use a steam cleaner to go over your carpets, upholstered surfaces, and furniture to kill dust mites safely and easily. 

4.     Wash frequently. Dust mites are difficult to eradicate completely, as they are small and undetectable to the naked eye. The best way to manage the situation and lower your risk of a reaction is to wash your bed linens and other fabrics at least once a week. Remember to wash and dry on high heat to ensure that they are completely gone.

5.     Use dust mite proof encasements. We are often most sensitive to dust mites that are in and around our beds. Reduce your exposure by encasing your mattress, pillow, and duvet in dust mite proof covers. The material of a good encasement will prevent dust mites from burrowing into the material and hiding.

3. Prevent another Dust Mite Infestation

  • Vacuuming frequently may cause dust mites to spread even more. When they are disturbed, they can become airborne and stay in the air for up to two hours, making it difficult to breath and causing an allergic reaction.
  • ‍You cannot see dust mites with your naked eye because they are extremely tiny and slightly translucent. However, you are able to collect a dust sample with some tape and inspect for feces appearing as small brown rectangular pellets.
  • Even with encasements and protectors, dust mite levels can still be high enough to cause discomfort. For this reason, it is still important to take the necessary steps to effectively manage dust mites.

Conclusion

Though it may take some time, you can eradicate your dust mite infestation. By following these steps and remaining vigilant, you will be able to control and eliminate your dust mite problem. Now equipped to succeed, go kill some dust mites—FAST!
