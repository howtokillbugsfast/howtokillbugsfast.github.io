How to Kill

BED BUGS

1. Identify the Problem

Finding the infestation can be challenging in and of itself. Bed bugs are small and difficult to find. The telltale sign is, of course, repeated bites in the night. You may notice itchy, raised bite marks.

Locate the infested area(s) by doing a visual inspection. A bright flashlight may be helpful for this step.

Correctly identify the bed bugs. Because they are so small, it can be difficult to be certain. Bed bugs are fairly easy to capture because they do not fly or jump, so you may want to capture the bug in a sealed plastic bag and show a pest control expert or a local extension agent for positive identification. Signs for bed bugs include:

  • Feces appearing brown to black in color and bumpy, adhering to surfaces
  • Bloodstains appearing as dark, rusty spots
  • Nymphs, cast skins, and shells (smaller in appearance and cream colored)
  • Tiny white eggs
  • A sweet, musty smell

Make sure you check everywhere in the infested room. Places you should be looking include, but are not limited to:

  • Your mattress, especially along the seams, piping, and all surfaces
  • Behind all furniture, headboards, picture frames, wallpaper and doors
  • In clocks, radios, and lamps
  • In dark corners, cracks, electrical outlets, suitcases, fabric seams, and stitching
  • Under the bed frame, carpets, and moldings

Inspect adjacent areas of your home for signs of bed bugs.

2. Treat the Infestation

Once you have located and contained the infested areas, you need to properly treat your home and belongings with an effective product and proper methods. Bed bugs can easily spread and re-infest a treated area if precautions are not taken.

  • Close off the infested area, ensuring that any items being removed from the area are properly sealed, doubled-bagged, and treated to prevent spread.
  • Reduce clutter by removing and disposing of items. This will reduce the number of places for bed bugs to hide.
  • Separate your launderable and non-launderable items. Place your belongings into separate bags and put them in the center of the room. This will make treatment easier and more effective, whether you do it yourself or hire a professional. 
  • Tape off any gaps or holes. Reduce the chance of bed bugs crawling under doors, through cracks in the wall, between glass panels, in air vents, etc. 

3. Prevent Re-Infestation

For continued success with your bed bug problem, it is a good idea for you to monitor the situation carefully for at least a few weeks. Because bed bugs are so small, they can be missed during treatment.

  • Zippered Mattress Encasements: Protect your mattress and yourself from bed bugs and bites. Bed bugs like to hide in the seams and creases of the material.
  • Insect Interceptors: Track bed bug activity around your bed and other furniture, and stop them in their tracks.
  • Desiccants: Diatomaceous earth or silica powder can be used to dehydrate and kill any bed bugs that may have been missed during initial treatment.

Conclusion

Though it may take some time, you can eradicate your bed bug infestation. By following these steps and remaining vigilant, you will be able to control and eliminate your bed bug problem. Now equipped to succeed, go kill some bed bugs—FAST!
